The project

A living record of Honmoku's urban forest

Honmoku is a quiet coastal neighbourhood in Yokohama — once an international settlement, now a place where old gardens and street trees carry decades of history in their rings.

This project documents those trees: species, height, canopy spread, and estimated age. Each entry is a small act of attention — a way of saying this tree was here, and someone noticed.

The map is open to everyone. Contributions come from a small group of approved members and are reviewed before going live.

How it works

Open to explore.
Reviewed before it's live.

Anyone can browse the map. Contributing data requires an approved account — and every submission is reviewed by the admin before it appears.

01

Explore the map

Browse every documented tree in Honmoku. Click any pin to see species, height, canopy data, and notes. No account needed.

02

Get a contributor account

Accounts are created manually by the project admin. Once approved, you receive credentials to sign in and contribute.

03

Submit a change

Add a new tree, edit an existing entry, or flag one for removal. Your change is queued for admin review before going live.

Free · read-only

Browse without an account

The full map is open to everyone. View species names, heights, canopy areas, and field notes for every documented tree in Honmoku.
